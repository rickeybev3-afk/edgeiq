2. What "n" means + priority order — two separate systems

"n" = number of historical backtest trades that passed that row's cumulative filter. The ladder is showing you quality improvement as you stack filters: 33,776 all trades → 5,856 at TCS ≥ 50 → 5,271 with IB < 10% → 2,852 with VWAP aligned.

The bot does have a separate priority ordering system, completely independent of that filter funnel:

P3 → Morning TCS 70+ (avg +7.58R — NEVER miss)
P1 → Intraday TCS 70+ (avg +4.44R)
P2 → Intraday TCS 50-69 (avg +2.15R)
P4 → Morning TCS 50-69 (avg +1.90R)
When multiple setups qualify on the same day, the bot sorts by P3→P1→P2→P4 and within each tier puts higher TCS first. These two systems work together: the filter funnel eliminates bad setups, and the priority tiers tell the bot which of the surviving good setups to act on first. They're not in conflict — the priority ordering operates within the already-filtered universe.
