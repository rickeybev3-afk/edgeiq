For years my journal was just entries, exits, and P&L. Looked productive. Wasn't helping. I was still bleeding money, so I dug deeper

I started tracking stuff that felt almost irrelevant:
- Day of the week
- What trade number it was (1st vs 4th of the day)
- How long I waited between trades
- Whether I rushed to my desk or eased into the session
- Performance after a winning streak vs losing streak
- Days I broke one rule vs days I broke multiple

After a few months I filtered the data. Some of it was uncomfortable to see:

Trade #1 vs Trade #4+: My first 1-2 trades had a solid win rate. By trade 4 the quality dropped off hard. The data showed me I wasn't finding more opportunities, I was forcing them. Borderline gambling honestly

Time between trades: When I took trades back-to-back with no pause, my win rate was horrible. Forcing myself to wait at least 5-10 min between trades helped me reset mentally instead of chasing or revenge trading. Honestly just getting up for 5 minutes to 'walk it off' helped me A LOT

Day of week: Wednesdays were my best. Tuesdays were a disaster for my strategy. So now most of the time, I'll fully skip trading Tuesdays, OR at the very least, size down. Saw an immediate improvement in my account

After a winning streak: I'd get cocky, size up, break rules. The day after a green day was often my worst. My brain wanted to keep the dopamine going

Rushed vs eased in: Days I woke up late and scrambled to my desk? Usually didn't go well. Days I gave myself 20-30 min before open to settle in, review levels, review rule checklist?
Way better results. Same setups, different headspace

Breaking one rule vs all of them: One broken rule I could recover from. But once I broke two, something clicked off in my brain and the whole day spiraled. Like my discipline was all-or-nothing. Now I have a hard rule: break one, I'm done for the day. Non-negotiable.
That safeguard alone saved me from some ugly drawdowns tbh

My core strategy stayed the same. The data just told me when to stay away from the charts. Once I built rules around that, things got a lot more consistent
