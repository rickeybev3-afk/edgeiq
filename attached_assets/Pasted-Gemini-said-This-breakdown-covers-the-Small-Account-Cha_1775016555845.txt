This breakdown covers the "Small Account Challenge" strategy used by Ross Cameron (Warrior Trading), which focuses on high-probability momentum trading. Since you're already trading in the $2−$20 range and focusing on cognitive performance, this "sniper" approach fits your profile well.

The Five Pillars of Stock Selection
For a small account (under $25,000), the criteria are much stricter than for a large account.

Pillar	Small Account Criteria	Rationale
Price	$0.25 to $9.00 Max	Maximizes share size and buying power.
% Change	50%+ Minimum	High momentum stocks have a higher probability of continuing.
RVOL	100+ Preferred	Indicates a massive supply/demand imbalance.
News	Required	A specific catalyst (FDA, 13D filing, etc.) justifies the price action.
Float	Sub-5M Preferred (20M Absolute Max)	Low float allows for "explosive" moves; avoids institutional Algos.
Trading Guidelines & "Guardrails"
The 3-Hour Window: Trade only from 7:00 AM to 10:00 AM. If no setup forms by 10:00 AM, walk away.

The "Front Side" Rule: Only trade the "front side" of the move—the period from the news release until the first MACD crossover.

Cash Account Strategy: Since cash takes time to settle, treat every trade as your "only" trade for the day. Be a sniper, not a machine gunner.

Math of Recovery: Avoid the "death spiral."

A 25% loss requires a 33% gain to break even.

A 50% loss requires a 100% gain to break even.

The Setup: The "First Candle to Make a New High"
This is the highest win-rate setup. It relies on the "Wave" principle of momentum.

Entry Checklist
The Surge: Stock hits the scanner, moving up on high volume (3–5 green candles).

The Pullback: Wait for 1–2 red candles on lower volume.

The Trigger: Enter the moment a candle breaks the high of the previous (pullback) candle.

Confirmation: Wait for the price to actually break the "Apex" rather than anticipating it.

Indicators to Watch
Level 2/Tape: Look for a "surge" of green orders.

MACD: Ensure the MACD lines are "open" and not crossing over yet.

Whole/Half Dollars: Expect resistance at $1.00 and $0.50 increments. Resistance usually flips to support once broken.

Volume Profile Integration
To incorporate this into your existing Volume Profile system, you should create a "Momentum Overlay" or a secondary tab that filters for these specific "Pillar" stocks.

Point of Control (POC) Shift: On these $2−$9 stocks, you want to see the POC shifting upward rapidly.

Low Volume Nodes (LVN): Use LVNs on the profile to set your profit targets. Usually, the stock will "zip" through LVNs and stall at High Volume Nodes (HVNs).

The "RVOL 100" Tab: Create a tab that specifically highlights stocks where the current volume is 100× the average volume for that time of day, filtered by your float requirements.
